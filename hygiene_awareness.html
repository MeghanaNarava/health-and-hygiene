Hygiene Awareness

1. Hand Hygiene: Hand washing is one of the most crucial personal hygiene practices. Regularly washing hands with an anti-bacterial soap for at least 20 seconds helps eliminate harmful bacteria and viruses, preventing the spread of illnesses.

2. Oral Hygiene: Taking care of your oral health involves brushing teeth at least twice a day, flossing regularly, and visiting the dentist for routine check-ups. Good oral hygiene prevents dental issues like cavities, gum diseases, and bad breath.

3. Body Hygiene: Showering or bathing daily helps keep the body clean, remove sweat, dirt, and bacteria, and prevent body odor. Using clean clothes and changing them regularly also falls under body hygiene.

4. Hair Hygiene: Regularly washing and grooming hair keeps it clean, prevents dandruff, and maintains a healthy scalp.

5. Nail Hygiene: Keeping nails short and clean reduces the risk of dirt and germs getting trapped underneath, which can lead to infections.

6. Clothing And Laundry Hygiene: Wearing clean clothes and washing them regularly at the appropriate temperature helps eliminate germs and maintain personal cleanliness.

7. Respiratory Hygiene: Covering the mouth and nose while coughing or sneezing can prevent the spread of respiratory droplets containing pathogens.

8. Intimate Hygiene: Special care should be taken to clean intimate areas gently and regularly to prevent infections and maintain comfort.

9. Period Hygiene: Wash your hands before and after using the bathroom. Also wash your hands before and after changing a tampon, pad, or menstrual cup.

10. Preventing Tooth Decay and Bad Breath: Brush your teeth twice a day with a soft toothbrush and fluoride toothpaste. Clean between your teeth with dental floss or interdental brushes every day. Having regular dental check-ups is also essential.
